I recently [wrote about wishing book clubs would work for me](/posts/2026/i-kind-of-wish-book-clubs-would-work-for-me/). I’m still not entirely sure they will, but I happened to notice that my local independent bookstore has a sci-fi book club and that they recently read [Children of Time](https://bookshop.org/p/books/children-of-time-adrian-tchaikovsky/854d8d3ca3125264). I was actually kind of sad missed that one as I loved that book, and the series as a whole so far as I’m reading the fourth book in the series, [Children of Strife](https://bookshop.org/p/books/children-of-strife-adrian-tchaikovsky/a5faafb97f58dfb0). I happened to notice that May’s book is [Star Wars: From a Certain Point of View](https://bookshop.org/p/books/from-a-certain-point-of-view-star-wars-meg-cabot/35b6a6d65cbee309). Being a Star Wars fan, it piqued my interest and since the library had it, I figured why not?
<!-- excerpt -->

So I’ll be giving it a try. I started the book the other night and enjoyed the few stories I read so far. The book club meets on the 24th, and I have the book checked out for two weeks, so I should be able to make it.

The downside is it will again cause me to pause reading *Children of Strife*, which is what has caused me to be reticent of joining any. I like being able to go through my backlog, however crookedly, and don’t always feel like I can squeeze in others. I don’t like reading too many books at one time since the concern of course is that I’m going to lose track of the plot and/or plot points in one or all of them while I bounce back and forth. This might also be a good test though since the Star Wars book is a collection of short stories rather than one long book, and it’s kind of about a movie I know and love.

I'm not sure how this is going to go, but after writing that last post, and seeing that the books that book have read had some overlap I started thinking that maybe I could try it once or twice and see how I can fit it into my reading habits. Stay tuned.
